Design notes.

Ok, right about now, the more sensitive of the designers are blowing chunks over their keyboards because of the blue and green and stripes. I'd like to remind people and Boughton that this is merely a testbed. I've added a couple of basic images so we can play around if we need some backgrounds, and I picked blue and green so you don't have to look at a grey screen. Meh.
I think that all the really basic styles the back-end designers need to start playing around are included, and therefore they can start messing with the page generating code. Further styles etc. will be added when they are needed, as will be form items, pictures for buttons etc. so it's not crucial at this point to worry about the colours.

Styles are listed by Item+sub-item (e.g. the post item has sub items - body, head, info etc.):

  1. Foldername
    The name of the folder. Black "Smallcaps" variant on a white background
  2. Threadname
    This style provides a blue background with a 1px black border and 2px padding.
  3. Messagefoot
    Named "message" to avoid confusion with the "post" items. It gives a dark blue background for the footer and page navigation box at the bottom of MainFrame
  4. Notifier
    Gray background, Green 10px font size, right float. An experimental item.
    The idea being that this item will be updated when you refresh the page, either by posting or by requesting new threads, (or perhaps as a timed event to avoid hammering the server), and it will simply read "N new posts to you" where N is the number of new posts addressed to you.
  5. Postnumber
    The number of the post (On delphi displayed at teh top of the message in the format of (xxx.2 in reply to xxx.1). Right justified, 10px fotn size.
  6. Postbody
    Provides the white background to the posts, with left, right and bottom 1px black border, and 2px padding to move the text away from the sides.
  7. Posthead
    Provides the blue background to the head of the post. Right, left and top borders at 1px.
    There is, annoyingly, a small gap between the cells, which I'm currently too tired to care about.
  8. Postinfo
    Information abou the post. Displayed on delphi as
    Message N of X
    Date
    Has a Right "float" value. This, combined with Posttofrom's left float vaue causes both items to share teh same line without the need to specify position either Absolutely or Relatively. Works in Netscape6, Opera6 and IE 6, so w00t.
  9. Posttofrom
    Who the post is To and who it's From. Has a left float value.
  10. Postresponse
    Sets 12px, centered, underlined text for te responce links: Reply, Delete, Ignore, Edit, etc. Underlining will be removed because it sucks.
